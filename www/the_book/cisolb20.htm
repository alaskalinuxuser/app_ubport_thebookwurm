The Promise of Christ

Read Isaiah 53 and Jeremiah 33. To understand these central chapters in the Old Testament dealing with Christ, it is important to consider other key Scriptures that focus on the line of Messianic promise.

  1. The seed of the woman
    The Messiah's earthly lineage can be traced through history.
    • Genesis 3:15 - "Her Seed... shall bruise thy head." Through the lineage of Adam.
    • Genesis 4:25 - "God... hath appointed me another seed." Through the lineage of Seth.
    • Genesis 9:9 - "I establish My covenant with you." Through the lineage of Noah.
    • Genesis 17:19 - "I will establish My covenant with him." Through Isaac, not Ishmael.
    • Genesis 32:28 - "Israel." Through the lineage of Jacob, renamed Israel.
    • Genesis 49:10 - "The sceptre shall not depart from Judah." Through the tribe of Judah.
    • 2Samuel 7:12 - "Thy seed after thee." Through the lineage of David.
    • Matthew 1:1-16 - "Jesus Christ the Son of David"
  2. A virgin's Son
    • Isaiah 7:14 - "A virgin shall... bear a Son... Immanuel." This name means "God with us."
    • Matthew 1:23 - "A virgin... Emmanuel"
  3. The Divine-Human King
    • Isaiah 9:6 - "A child is born... the mighty God"
    • Isaiah 9:7 - "Government... upon the throne of David"
    • Isaiah 11:1 - "Of the stem of Jesse." The father of David.
    • Isaiah 11:2-12 - "The spirit of the Lord shall rest upon Him." Messiah's kingdom is described (see also Isaiah 2:1-5).
    • Jeremiah 33:14-26 - "The Branch of righteousness... unto David"
  4. The humiliation and salvation of Christ
    • Isaiah 53:3 - "Despised and rejected of men"
    • Matthew 27:27-35 - "Mocked... spit upon... crucified"
    • Isaiah 53:5 - "Wounded... bruised... for our iniquities"
    • Isaiah 53:6 - "Laid on Him the iniquity of us all"
    • Isaiah 53:10 - "Soul and offering for sin"
    • Matthew 26:38 - "Exceeding sorrowful, even unto death"
    • Matthew 27:46 - "My God, My God, why?"
