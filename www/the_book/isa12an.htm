Isaiah 12 - Outline of Isaiah (MENU page)
(6.) The King: the Salvation of His people (12:1-3)
1. And in that day thou shalt say, O LORD, I will praise thee:
though thou wast angry with me, thine anger is turned away, {cp. 10:4,25}
and thou comfortedst me. {40:1}
2 Behold, God [is] my salvation; I will trust, and not be afraid:
for the LORD JEHOVAH [is] my strength and [my] song;
he also is become my salvation.
3 Therefore with joy shall ye draw water out of the wells of salvation.
the LORD JEHOVAH... is become my salvation {HB= yeshuah (all 3 occurrences in v.2,3)}. -
Observe that salvation is not merely provided by God, nor is it obtained through religious rituals or education. Salvation is in the Person of God Himself.
Verse 2 quotes from Psa 118:14,21,22 (which is a Psalm of the Messiah.)
'Yeshua,' transliterated into Greek, is 'Jesus.' cp. Mat 1:21; Acts 4:10-12
water out of the wells of salvation.- cp. Isa 55:1; Joh 4:10; 7:37; Rev 7:17; 22:1,17
'Wells' speak of abundance, satisfaction and refreshing for the believing heart.
This prophecy speaks of the fulfillment of the feast of Tabernacles, for the Lord will dwell among His people (v.6). In the days of the second Temple, during the celebration of the feast of Tabernacles, the priests would draw water from the pool of Siloam, carry it to the Temple, and pour it out at the altar as they recited v.3. That was the occasion of Jesus' words in Joh 7:37-39. (See the Book Notes at John 7:37-39)
 
(7.) The King: His Praise, the song of all people (12:4-6)
4. And in that day shall ye say, Praise the LORD,
call upon his name, declare his doings among the people,
make mention that his name is exalted.
5 Sing unto the LORD;
for he hath done excellent things: this [is] known in all the earth.
6 Cry out and shout, thou inhabitant of Zion:
for great [is] the Holy One of Israel in the midst of thee.
praise the LORD...- cp. Rev 15:3; 19:1,2
...thou inhabitant of Zion... - cp. Psa 132:13-18
great is the Holy One of Israel in the midst of thee.- How great?
Note the identity of the King, in the midst of His people, as described in Zeph 3:14-17; Zech 2:5,10,11
cry out and shout...- The expression of joy in the LORD's Presence,
neither reserved nor timid, bursts forth from each of the redeemed, welling up from "all that is within me." Psa 103:1
